# SOPs — PVS ClearView

Window cleaning, gutter cleaning, pressure washing, soft washing, junk
removal. Read alongside the company-wide SOPs.

---

## Service standards

A finished ClearView visit means:
- All scope items completed per the work order
- No streaks, smudges, or fingerprints on glass we touched
- Sills wiped and dry
- Gutters: debris removed by hand, downspouts flushed clear
- Pressure / soft wash: surface uniformly clean, plantings rinsed,
  no detergent residue
- All ladders, hoses, equipment loaded back in the truck
- Customer entryways unlocked the way we found them

## Pre-route checklist (every morning)

- [ ] Squeegees clean, rubber in good shape (rotate worn ones)
- [ ] Microfibre cloths (clean, dry, divided by surface)
- [ ] Window-cleaning solution mixed and labeled
- [ ] Bucket and applicator
- [ ] Extension pole (water-fed for upper floors if booked)
- [ ] Ladders sized for the job (8' + extension, both)
- [ ] Pressure washer fueled if booked
- [ ] Soft-wash applicator + biodegradable detergent if booked
- [ ] Gloves, eye protection, gutter scoop, garbage bags
- [ ] Tarp for catching debris under gutters

## Window cleaning procedure

**Interior:**
1. Move or cover anything below the window (electronics, soft furniture).
2. Spray solution onto microfibre, not the window. Wipe sills and frame
   to remove grit before squeegee.
3. Squeegee top to bottom in overlapping passes.
4. Detail edges with a dry microfibre.
5. Wipe sill dry.
6. Replace anything you moved.

**Exterior:**
1. Walk and inspect frames before starting — note any damaged seals or
   cracked glass and flag to the customer.
2. Pre-rinse if heavily soiled (pollen, bird droppings).
3. Apply solution with applicator pole or sprayer.
4. Squeegee or water-fed brush on second-storey + up.
5. Detail edges.
6. Final rinse if water-fed (deionised water leaves no spots).

**Quality check:** Stand back 6 feet, look at every window. Streak =
re-do. The streak-free guarantee is the brand — it's not negotiable.

## Gutter cleaning procedure

1. Tarp the ground below the gutter run.
2. Ladder set per ladder safety (1:4 ratio, top extending 3 rungs
   above contact point, tied off where possible).
3. Hand-scoop debris into a bucket. Don't drop debris from the ladder
   — it splatters and ruins the rinse later.
4. Flush every downspout from the top with the hose. Confirm flow at
   the bottom. If blocked, snake or remove the elbow to clear.
5. Visual-inspect every gutter section for: loose fasteners, sagging,
   sealant gaps, rust holes, downspout damage.
6. Note repairs needed → photo + flag the customer with a free quote
   for the fix.
7. Rinse the gutters from the outside if heavily stained (separate
   service — quote, don't include free).
8. Pack out all debris in bags. Nothing in customer's bin without
   permission.

## Pressure washing procedure

- ALWAYS test in a hidden corner first — different surfaces handle
  different pressure
- Driveway / concrete: 2500–3000 PSI, surface cleaner attachment
- Wood deck: soft wash or low-pressure (≤ 1200 PSI), wide fan tip,
  wash with the grain
- Brick / paver: medium pressure, surface cleaner for flat runs
- Sand-set pavers will lose jointing sand — quote re-sand if customer
  wants the polished look
- Always pre-rinse plantings. Cover with tarp if detergent will hit.
- Post-rinse plantings again

## Soft-wash (house wash) procedure

- For siding, soffit, stucco
- Low pressure + biodegradable detergent (manufacturer-rated for
  exterior)
- Apply bottom up (prevents streaks), rinse top down
- Dwell time: 5–10 minutes; don't let it dry on the surface
- Watch for wind drift onto cars, neighbours, plants
- Final walkaround — look for missed spots in raking light

## Junk removal / property cleanout procedure

1. Quote in person when possible. Photos for remote quotes.
2. Confirm scope before loading: what stays, what goes.
3. Separate as you load:
   - Donations (Goodwill, Salvation Army, local thrift)
   - Recyclables (metal, e-waste, cardboard)
   - Landfill
4. Treat the property like our own — no scuffs on walls, no marks on
   floors. Use moving blankets.
5. Hazardous items (paint, propane, batteries, chemicals) → DO NOT
   accept. Flag the customer to municipal hazardous waste day.
6. End-of-job photo of the cleared space.
7. Itemised donation/recycling receipt to the customer if they want
   it for tax purposes.

## Safety standards (ClearView is highest-risk division)

- **Ladders:** 3-point contact at all times. No reaching beyond your
  belt buckle line. Tie off when possible.
- **Heights over 1 storey:** spotter required at the base.
- **Roof access:** approved roof shoes only, never on wet/icy roof.
  Harness + tie-off on any roof over 4/12 pitch.
- **Pressure washer:** never aim at people, animals, electrical, or
  windows.
- **Electrical:** treat all overhead lines and wet receptacles as live.
  10-foot clearance from any line.
- **Solo work prohibited** for any job involving an extension ladder
  or a roof.

## Quality red flags (stop and call Office)

- Cracked or compromised window glass — don't clean, don't touch, photo
- Loose gutter pulling away from fascia — risk of collapse
- Soft / rotted fascia or soffit
- Active wasp / hornet nest in gutter or eaves
- Power line within 10 feet of work area
- Roof too steep / icy / slippery for safe access

# Annual Business Calendar

Month-by-month plan for the whole year — operations, marketing,
financial, team. Print this. Put it on the office wall. Block these
in your calendar at the start of each year.

---

## How to read this

Each month has four lanes:

- **Ops** — what crews are doing
- **Sales / Marketing** — what's running, what to plan
- **Financial / Admin** — close-out and planning
- **Team / Owner** — hiring, training, your own work

The dates are guidance — adjust to your actual season. The
**rhythm** is what matters.

## January — Quiet month, strategy heavy

### Ops
- SnowLand active — peak winter response season
- ClearView + LawnPros off-season (minor commercial windows + interior
  jobs if available)
- Equipment maintenance for non-snow gear

### Sales / Marketing
- 🎯 **Renew all spring contracts** — early-bird pricing offer
  ($50 off if booked by Feb 15)
- Send "thank you 2024" cards to top 25 customers
- GBP post: "Winter ready — book your spring cleanup now"
- Plan next 12 months of social content themes

### Financial / Admin
- ⭐ **Close prior year books** with accountant
- Issue T4s (deadline Feb 28) — payroll provider handles
- Review prior year P&L, set this year's revenue target
- Update pricing for the new year (5-10% increase typical)
- Insurance renewal review

### Team / Owner
- Annual all-team meeting — review prior year, share goals
- Crew lead annual reviews + raise conversations
- Block strategic planning weekend for Q1
- Personal: book a vacation for the slow part of Q3

- Personal: do not skip rest days — burnout starts here

| Wednesday Office sync | Wednesdays 12:00pm | 30 min |

| Quarterly planning weekend | First weekend of new quarter | 1 day |
| Annual strategic week | Mid-December | 1 week (off-grid) |
| Marketing batch (1 month of content) | First Monday of month | 3 hr |
| Inventory check | First of month | 30 min |
| Vacation block 1 | Late May long weekend | 4 days |
| Vacation block 2 | Late June | 1 week |
| Vacation block 3 | Late September | long weekend |
| Vacation block 4 | Late December | 1 week (with strategic week) |

## What to AVOID putting on your calendar

- Routine customer calls (that's Office's job)
- Dispatch decisions (Operations Manager or Office)
- Routine invoicing (Office)
- Running a route (only when teaching, not as default)
- Equipment purchases under $200 (Office or crew lead authority)

Every block on the calendar should be:
- A rhythm (recurring time you spend on the business)
- A leverage activity (hire, price, system, strategy)
- A relationship (key customer, key vendor, mentor)
- Or your own life (family, health, vacation)

If it's none of those — it shouldn't be on your calendar.

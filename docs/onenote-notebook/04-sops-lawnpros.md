# SOPs — PVS LawnPros

Division-specific procedures for lawn care work. Read alongside the
company-wide SOPs.

---

## Service standards

A finished LawnPros visit means:
- Lawn cut at the agreed height (default: 3.5" summer, 2.5" spring/fall)
- Crisp string-trim edges along beds, walkways, drives, fences
- Hard-edge along sidewalks and driveways if booked
- Clippings bagged, mulched, or blown back per customer preference
- Walkways, drive, and patio blown clean
- No equipment marks, ruts, or scalped spots
- Gate closed and latched on the way out

---

## Pre-route checklist (every morning)

- [ ] Mower blades sharp (rotate sharp blades in weekly)
- [ ] String trimmer line loaded
- [ ] Backpack blower fuel + 2-stroke mix
- [ ] Gas can full (regular + mix)
- [ ] Spare trimmer line bottle
- [ ] Tarp + bags for clippings
- [ ] First-aid kit in the truck
- [ ] Phone charged for photos

---

## Mowing technique standards

- Alternate mowing direction each visit (stripe pattern improves turf
  health and visual finish)
- Slow down on turns near beds and trees — no scalping, no bark damage
- Single-pass on healthy lawns; double-pass on overgrown first visits
- Wet lawn: bagger only (mulching wet clippings clumps). Reschedule if
  cutting would damage turf
- Steep slopes: walk-behind only, never riding mower

---

## Edging standards

- String-trim everywhere the mower deck can't reach: fence lines, posts,
  AC units, base of trees, mailbox posts
- Hard-edge sidewalks + driveways monthly minimum (every visit if
  customer is on the premium plan)
- Don't trim into garden beds — that's bed-edging, a separate service

---

## Spring cleanup procedure

Pre-season visit (first appointment of the year for most customers).

1. Walk the property with the customer (or alone if not home).
2. Rake or blow leaves and debris from the lawn and beds.
3. De-thatch lightly if matting is visible.
4. Edge beds and along walkways.
5. First mow at the highest acceptable setting (don't shock the lawn).
6. Bag and haul all debris to the truck.
7. Blow walkways, drive, patio.
8. Photo: full before and after, plus one detail shot of bed-edging.
9. Hand the customer (or door-hanger) a list of recommended add-ons:
   aeration, overseeding, dethatching, mulch refresh.

---

## Aeration procedure

- Best window: spring or fall (avoid hot, dry summer)
- Mark sprinkler heads, septic lids, dog fences before starting
- Core-pull aerator only (spike aerators do less)
- Even, full-coverage passes (overlap 25%)
- Leave the cores on the lawn — they break down naturally
- Recommend overseeding the same visit if turf is thin

---

## Quality red flags (stop and call Office)

- Severe pest damage (chinch, grubs) visible — recommend an inspection
- Septic field bouncing under the mower — soil is saturated; reschedule
- Underground sprinkler visible (broken / surfaced) — don't mow over,
  flag the customer
- Wildlife sign you don't recognise — bear scat, large nest, etc.

---

## Customer interaction standards

- Greet by name. The route sheet has it — use it.
- Never give pricing on add-ons without dispatch approval beyond your
  $300 authority.
- If asked to do something outside scope: *"Happy to take a look —
  let me call the office and we'll send you a quote today."*
- If the customer is upset: don't argue. Listen, apologise, take notes,
  call dispatch.

---

## Weather rules

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Light rain, lawn still firm | Continue |
| Heavy rain | Reschedule — text customer by 7am |
| Saturated lawn (sinking) | Reschedule even if not raining |
| Lightning visible / thunder audible | Stop work, get to the truck, wait 30 min after last thunder |
| Heat advisory > 35°C | Start by 6am, finish by noon, helper to monitor lead for heat signs |

---

## End-of-visit photo standards

Every job, three photos minimum:

1. **Before**: full property frame
2. **After**: same angle, post-mow
3. **Detail**: one crisp shot of the edging or stripe pattern

Upload before leaving the property. Photos are how we prove quality
when a customer disputes — and how we sell the next customer.

# KPIs & Scorecard

What to measure, where to get it, what "good" looks like, and what to
do when a number goes red.

The point of metrics is **decisions, not vanity**. If a number won't
change what you do this week, don't track it.

---

## The principle

Owners drown in numbers because they try to track everything.
Pick a few. Update weekly. Act on the reds. Adjust quarterly.

> *"The number that gets watched gets managed. The number that gets
> reported quarterly gets ignored."*

---

## The dashboard — one spreadsheet, one page

Build a Google Sheet or Excel file titled "PVS Scorecard." One row
per week. Columns for each KPI. Color-coded conditional formatting
(green / yellow / red).

Put a calendar reminder for Friday 4pm to update it.

---

## Weekly scorecard (5 numbers — 10 min Friday)

These move every week. They're the early warning system.

### 1. Revenue this week
- **Source:** Invoicing system / CRM
- **Target:** Set each January, broken into weekly chunks
- **Red:** > 15% below plan two weeks in a row
- **Why it matters:** Earliest signal of demand or capacity issue

### 2. Jobs completed this week
- **Source:** Dispatch tool
- **Target:** Capacity-based (crews × days × jobs/day)
- **Red:** Below capacity + revenue below plan = under-booked
- **Why it matters:** Capacity utilization. Underbooked = sales
  problem; overbooked + unhappy customers = need to hire.

### 3. New leads this week + source
- **Source:** CRM, with source tag (Google, referral, drive-by, etc.)
- **Target:** Set monthly based on capacity targets
- **Red:** < 5 leads/week for sustained period
- **Why it matters:** Top of funnel. Adjust marketing if leads dry up.

### 4. Quotes sent + close rate
- **Source:** CRM
- **Target:** 50-65% close rate. Lower = pricing too high. Higher =
  too low.
- **Red:** Close rate falls below 40% suddenly
- **Why it matters:** Pricing health + sales effectiveness

### 5. Cash position
- **Source:** Bank app
- **Target:** Always 1+ month of operating expenses
- **Red:** Below 2x next week's payroll
- **Why it matters:** Survival metric. Everything else is academic if
  cash runs out.

## Monthly scorecard (7 more numbers — 30 min last Friday)

These move slower but matter strategically.

### 6. New customers this month
- **Source:** CRM, count of first invoice this month
- **Target:** Set annually
- **Red:** Two consecutive months below plan

### 7. Customer churn / pause rate
- **Source:** CRM, count of recurring customers who paused or
  cancelled this month
- **Target:** < 5% per month for recurring; < 10% for one-time after
  one year
- **Red:** > 10% in any month → investigate cause

### 8. Average customer LTV (lifetime value)
- **Source:** Total revenue per customer / customers
- **Target:** $800+ Year 1, growing to $2,500+ Year 3
- **Red:** Falling LTV = churning before they get value

### 9. Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- **Source:** Total marketing spend / new customers acquired
- **Target:** < $50 Year 1, < $30 Year 3
- **Red:** CAC growing faster than LTV → unsustainable

### 10. Google review count + average rating
- **Source:** GBP
- **Target:** 2 new reviews/week minimum; maintain 4.8+ average
- **Red:** No new reviews for 2+ weeks; rating drops below 4.7

### 11. Recurring revenue %
- **Source:** Recurring revenue / total revenue
- **Target:** 50% Year 1, 80% Year 3
- **Red:** Below 40% — too one-time-job-dependent, fragile

### 12. Net profit margin %
- **Source:** P&L (net profit / revenue)
- **Target:** 10-15% Year 1, 20-25% Year 3
- **Red:** Below 10% sustained → pricing problem OR cost problem;
  drill into the P&L

## How to react to a red

The temptation is to panic. Don't.

### One-week red
Note it. Watch it. Don't act yet — one week is noise.

### Two-week red
Stop and investigate. 30-minute root cause:
- What changed? (Weather, seasonal, competitor, internal)
- Is it the metric or the underlying business?
- Talk to Office / Operations Manager — what are they seeing?

### Three-week red
Action plan. Specific, owner-assigned, deadline.

### One-month red on a strategic metric
Add to next quarterly planning as a focused rock.

---

## Anti-metrics — what NOT to track

These look meaningful but mislead:

- **Hours worked by you** — long hours is a symptom, not a goal
- **Number of customers** without revenue context (1000 $10 customers
  is worse than 100 $1000 customers)
- **Social media followers** without engagement
- **Website traffic** without conversion
- **"Busy"-ness** — feeling busy ≠ progress

If a number doesn't tie back to revenue, profit, customer health,
or capacity — it's vanity.

## The scorecard ritual

The scorecard is worthless if you don't look at it.

- **Friday 4pm** — update weekly numbers (10 min)
- **Last Friday of month** — update monthly numbers (30 min)
- **First Friday of new quarter** — quarterly review (90 min)
- **Mid-December** — full annual review

The ritual is the point. The numbers update; the discipline of
looking at them is what changes the business.

# Customer-Facing Scripts

Use these as starting points, not memorized lines. Customers can hear
a script — they can't hear a person who knows what they're doing.

Read each one out loud once before going live. Adjust word choice to
your natural voice. Keep the structure.

---

## Phone intake (inbound new lead)

### Opening
> *"Prestige View Services — this is {name}. How can I help?"*

### Discovery (let them talk first; take notes)
- *"Got it — what's the property address?"*
- *"Is this a one-time job, or are you looking for something on a
  regular schedule?"*
- *"How soon are you hoping to get started?"*
- *"How did you hear about us?"* (always — track the channel)

### Recap to confirm
> *"So just to confirm — you're at {address}, looking for
> {service}, ideally starting {timeframe}. Anything I missed?"*

### Next step
> *"I'll have a quote in your inbox by end of day. Best email?
> Once you've had a look, give us a call back or just reply to
> the email to confirm and we'll book you in."*

### Close
> *"Thanks for calling — talk soon."*

## Quote follow-up call (2 business days after sending)

### Opening
> *"Hi {name}, it's {your name} from Prestige View Services. I'm
> following up on the quote we sent you Monday for the
> {service} at {address}. Did it come through?"*

### If they say yes but haven't decided
> *"No pressure at all — just wanted to check if you had any
> questions. Some folks like to walk the property with us before
> deciding, others want to think it over. What works for you?"*

### If they say the price is too high
> *"That feedback is fair. Mind if I ask what number you were
> hoping to land at? Sometimes I can adjust scope — for example,
> mowing every other week instead of weekly, or doing the
> exterior windows only — to fit a different budget."*

### If they've decided to go with someone else
> *"Totally understand. Can I ask what tipped it? Just trying to
> learn so we do better next time. And — if they don't work out,
> we'll be here."*

### If they're ready to book
> *"Awesome. I'll send you a quick confirmation email with the
> first visit date. We'll text you the morning of with our ETA.
> Anything we should know about the property — gates, dogs, alarm?"*

---

## Quote rejection — Lost-reason capture

When a lead says no, ALWAYS ask why. One sentence in the Lost-reason
field is product gold. Common categories:

- Price (capture: what would have closed it?)
- Timing (capture: when should we follow up?)
- Going with relative / friend
- Already have another company
- Scope mismatch (we don't offer what they wanted)

Office reviews Lost-reasons monthly. Patterns become quote-template
updates, pricing reviews, or new services.

## On-site arrival (crew lead)

### When customer is home
*Knock once, step back from the door.*

> *"Hi, I'm {name} from Prestige View Services — we're here for
> your {service} today. Just wanted to let you know we're starting.
> Anything you'd like me to take a look at before we get going?"*

### When customer is not home
*Doorbell once if it's clearly a residence; otherwise just start.*

Leave a door-hanger after the job with the completion notice.

---

## On-site mid-job interaction

### Customer comes out mid-job
> *"Hey there — just letting you know we're about halfway through.
> Should be wrapped up in about {time}. Holler if anything comes up."*

### Customer asks about add-on work
> *"Yeah, happy to take a look. Let me snap a photo and have the
> office send you a quote today — they'll know the right price
> better than I can off the top of my head."*

### Customer offers a tip
> *"Really appreciate that — but the team gets that built in. The
> best thanks is a Google review, if you've got a minute later.
> I'll text you the link."*

---

## End-of-job handoff

### Customer is home
> *"All wrapped up. The {area} is back open. Anything you noticed
> you'd like us to flag for next time?"*

*(Then if satisfied:)*

> *"If we did right by you today, the biggest thing you can do is
> leave us a quick Google review — I'll send you the link in a few
> minutes. Takes 30 seconds and it really helps."*

### Customer is not home

Leave a door-hanger with:
- Date of service
- Service performed
- Crew name + crew lead name
- "Satisfaction guaranteed — call us within 48 hours if anything's
  not right"
- Phone number

Office texts the customer within 2 hours of job completion with the
review link.

## Objection handling

### "Your quote is higher than the other guy."
> *"Fair — we're not always the cheapest. What you're paying for is
> a fully insured crew, photo documentation of every visit, and a
> guaranteed callback if anything's off. Most of our customers end
> up with us long-term because they stop having to chase. Want me
> to walk you through what's included so you can compare apples to
> apples?"*

### "Why so much more for the recurring plan than one-time?"
> *"It's actually the opposite — recurring is cheaper per visit. The
> one-time price covers our trip out, set-up time, and a one-off
> margin. Recurring locks you in a route, so we're already in your
> neighbourhood. That's where the savings come from."*

### "Can I get a discount if I pay cash?"
> *"I appreciate the offer but we run everything through proper
> invoicing for tax and warranty purposes. What I can do is bundle
> services — for example, window cleaning AND gutter cleaning on
> the same visit saves on the trip. Want me to put a bundle quote
> together?"*

### "Why do I need insurance proof? I trust you."
> *"Means a lot, thank you. The reason we offer the cert upfront is
> for your protection — if anything happens on the property, our
> insurance covers it, not your home policy. Some folks don't ask
> for it; we just include it standard."*

### "Are you the owner?"
> *"I run the crew, and the owner's {name} — based right here in
> Petawawa. If you ever need to escalate something, the office can
> get you straight to {name} same day."*

### "How long have you been in business?"
> *"We're a newer name but the crew has years on the equipment.
> What's new is the company structure — we built PVS specifically
> to give the Valley a year-round, insured, modernised team
> instead of juggling multiple contractors. That's the difference."*

### "I want to talk to my spouse before deciding."
> *"Of course — happy to send the quote to both emails so you've
> both got it in writing. When's a good time to follow up?"*

### "Just send the quote and I'll get back to you."
> *"Will do — quote's in your inbox by end of day. I'll check in
> Wednesday if I haven't heard back, just to make sure it didn't
> get buried. Sound fair?"*

### "I'll do it myself."
> *"Totally respect that — most of what we do, you can do yourself
> if you've got the time and the gear. Where most customers land
> with us is recurring service so they don't have to think about
> it. If you ever change your mind, you've got our number."*

## Complaint / negative review response

### In-person or phone complaint
1. Listen completely. No interrupting.
2. *"I hear you — I'm really sorry that happened. Walk me through
   what we'd need to do to make this right for you."*
3. Take notes. Repeat back what you heard.
4. Offer a remedy within your authority (re-work, credit, refund).
5. If beyond your authority: *"I want to make sure the owner sees
   this directly — can I get them on the phone now, or is later
   today better?"*
6. Follow up in writing within 24 hours with the resolution.

### Negative public review response (Google)

Respond within 24 hours. Format:

> *"Hi {name} — this is {Owner name}, owner of Prestige View
> Services. I'm sorry to hear about your experience. I'd like to
> understand what happened and make it right. Could you give me a
> call directly at (613) 334-5858, or email contact@prestigeviewservices.ca?
> We take feedback like this seriously."*

NEVER argue publicly. NEVER blame the customer. NEVER blame the
crew. The public response is a signal to OTHER readers that the
business is mature. Resolve the actual issue privately.

## Follow-up cadence for inactive customers

After 60 days of no contact with a recurring customer:

> *"Hi {name}, it's {office name} from PVS. Just touching base —
> we haven't seen you on the route lately. Everything OK? Want to
> get you back on the schedule, or pause for a while? Either way,
> just let me know."*

After 6 months inactive — light reactivation offer:

> *"Hi {name}, hope you're well. Spring season's right around the
> corner — wanted to see if you'd like us back on the schedule for
> {service}. First visit's 15% off as a welcome-back. No pressure
> either way."*

# Start Here — Owner's Orientation

Welcome. This is your operating manual. Not your employees' — yours.

The rest of this notebook is split in two:

- **Owner Track (00–13, 14–23)** — how to run the company, plan the
  year, market it, finance it, and step back from the day-to-day
- **Operator Track (03–10)** — how the work actually gets done in the
  divisions (your team uses these)

Read the Owner Track first. It's everything you need to work **on** the
business instead of **in** it.

---

## The one-line philosophy

> *"Build the company so it can run a week without you. Then a month.
> Then a quarter. That's the goal — not a bigger to-do list."*

Every section in this notebook is in service of that goal. If a
process needs you, it's not a process — it's a job. Document it,
delegate it, and move on to the next leverage point.

## How to actually use this notebook

### Week 1 — Read everything in the Owner Track
~6 hours total. Take notes in the margins (or as OneNote comments).
Don't try to act on it all. Just absorb.

### Week 2 — Build your dashboard
Open `18-kpis-scorecard.md`. Pick the 5 numbers you'll start
tracking weekly. Build a one-page spreadsheet. Update it every
Friday.

### Week 3 — Block your owner calendar
Open `14-owner-operating-system.md`. Block your daily, weekly,
monthly rhythms in your calendar. Make them recurring.

### Week 4 — Run a full owner rhythm
Do every rhythm — daily, weekly, monthly close — exactly once. See
where it breaks. Fix the calendar block, fix the dashboard, fix the
notebook.

### Month 2 onwards — One section per week
Each week, pick one Owner Track section, re-read it, and improve
one thing. Update the markdown. Commit the change. Within a quarter
the notebook reflects how YOU run YOUR business — not the template.

### Quarterly — Strategic planning weekend
Block one Saturday + Sunday per quarter. Use `19-strategic-planning.md`.
Set 3 rocks for the next 90 days. Review the previous quarter's
rocks honestly.

### Annually — Vision + numbers + people review
Block one full week off in December (or your slowest month). Use
`01-vision.md`, `17-financial-management.md`, `20-hiring-management.md`.
Update the 1/3/5-year goals. Set the next year's revenue target.
Plan hires.

## The trap to avoid

Most owner-operators read a book like *The E-Myth Revisited* or
*Built to Sell*, get fired up for a week, then drop back into
firefighting. The notebook can sit pretty and do nothing.

The way out is the **calendar**, not the **intention**.

If your weekly KPI review isn't a recurring 30-minute block at the
same time every week, it won't happen. If your quarterly planning
weekend isn't booked off in your calendar a year in advance, it
won't happen. If your daily 6:30am numbers review isn't sacred,
it won't happen.

Put the rhythms in the calendar this week. Then this notebook
becomes the actual operating system. Without the calendar, it's
just a document.

## The four leverage points (where your time pays off most)

1. **Hiring the next person well** — one great hire frees 20+ hours
   per week of your time
2. **Pricing changes** — a 5% price increase often goes to the
   bottom line entirely
3. **Marketing system that runs without you** — referrals + GBP +
   recurring contracts compound while you sleep
4. **A clear standard so the team can self-correct** — every SOP in
   the Operator Track is leverage; the more you write, the less you
   field calls

When you have an hour of "owner time," spend it on one of these
four. Not on a mowing route. Not on rebuilding the dispatch board.
Not on chasing a $200 invoice. Owner time goes to leverage.

## When in doubt, ask:

> *"Am I the only person who can do this?
> If yes — am I doing it because I LOVE it, or because I haven't
> hired / trained / documented it yet?"*

If you love it, keep it. The rest is delegation runway.

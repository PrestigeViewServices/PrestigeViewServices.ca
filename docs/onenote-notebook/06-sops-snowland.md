# SOPs — PVS SnowLand

Residential snow plowing, walkway clearing, ice control. Read alongside
the company-wide SOPs.

---

## Service standards

A finished SnowLand visit means:
- Driveway cleared full-width down to asphalt or compacted base
- Snow piled away from sight lines (corner of property, never blocking
  views at the end of the drive)
- Walkways shoveled curb-to-door, salted if included
- No snow pushed into the customer's lawn beds (kills turf in spring)
- Mailbox area cleared
- Garbage / recycling pickup area cleared on collection days
- No damage to driveway markers, garage doors, fences, or beds

---

## Seasonal pre-storm checklist (October every year)

- [ ] All trucks fitted with plows; blades inspected
- [ ] Salt and sand stocked at HQ + each crew lead's home base
- [ ] All trucks have shovels, brooms, ice chippers, headlamps
- [ ] Backup blowers ready
- [ ] Driveway markers installed at every contract property (every
      visit, the customer sees we were there even with fresh snow)
- [ ] Route maps updated for the season
- [ ] All customers contacted: confirm contract, address, gate codes,
      special instructions

---

## Pre-storm activation

**Trigger:** Forecast calls for 2+ cm or freezing rain within 24 hours.

1. Owner / Operations sends crew lead the activation text by 6pm.
2. Crew lead confirms equipment, fuel, salt stock, helper availability.
3. Office sends a "we're on it — your driveway is in the route" text
   to all contract customers.

---

## During-storm procedure

| Snowfall | Action |
|---|---|
| 0–2 cm | Walkway clearing only if booked, no plow run |
| 2–7 cm | Single plow pass at storm end |
| 7–15 cm | Mid-storm pass + final cleanup pass |
| 15+ cm | Continuous coverage; multiple passes through the route |
| Freezing rain | Salt route — no plowing needed unless followed by snow |

- Priority customers: routes that lead to early-shift workers
  (CFB Petawawa starts 6am — those driveways done first)
- Final cleanup pass is mandatory before sign-off
- Walkway clearing always last (cleaner the customer sees first)

---

## Plowing technique standards

- Approach the driveway slowly. Read the lay before pushing.
- Push snow to the side of the property opposite the front door
  whenever possible.
- Never push snow across the road or onto a neighbour's lawn.
- Watch for driveway markers, lights, paving stones, sprinkler heads.
- If you can't see the edge — get out and walk it. Equipment damage
  is on us; we eat the cost.
- Garage doors: stop 24" short. Don't push snow up against the door
  (freezes shut, scratches paint).

---

## Walkway / salt procedure

1. Shovel walkway curb-to-door, single shovel-width minimum, two-width
   on premium plans.
2. Clear steps fully — top, sides, treads.
3. Apply salt or grit per customer plan:
   - Standard: hand-applied salt on icy patches only
   - Premium: full salt application every visit
4. Don't oversalt — it pits concrete and kills plants. Light, even
   coverage.
5. Mailbox approach — clear a 3-foot box around the mailbox.

---

## Quality red flags (stop and call Office)

- Vehicle parked in the driveway with no contact info — don't push
  around it without confirming with the customer
- New driveway extension or paving stones not on the route sheet
- Visible damage to the property from a prior pass (scrape, rut,
  broken marker) — document and flag immediately
- Power outage at the property — note for customer awareness
- Tree or branch down — note and offer follow-up cleanup quote

---

## Customer interaction during a storm

- Customers WILL call asking when you're coming. Office handles
  these calls — crews stay focused on the route.
- If a customer approaches in person:
  *"You're in the route, you'll be done before 7am. Call the office
   if you don't see us by then."*
- Never promise a specific time mid-storm. Promise the contract window.

---

## Storm wrap-up procedure

1. Final route pass within 4 hours of last flake.
2. Walk each property visually — anything missed?
3. Take post-storm photo of each driveway. Stored as proof.
4. Crew lead end-of-storm summary text to Owner:
   - Storm total (cm)
   - Number of passes
   - Total hours run
   - Salt + fuel used
   - Any damage, incidents, or customer flags
5. Office files all photos against the customer's account.

---

## Off-season (May–October)

- Equipment maintenance month (May): plows off trucks, blades
  inspected, mounting hardware checked, sandblasted + repainted
  every 2 years
- Storage: plows stored under cover, salt in sealed bins
- Sales push (Sep–Oct): new snow contracts sold, last year's customers
  re-contracted with locked-in pricing
- Crew rotates to other divisions (LawnPros / ClearView)

---

## Insurance + liability reminders

- Every snow contract specifies a maximum response window — we own
  that promise. Missing it without notice = potential liability
  if a customer slips.
- Salt application is recommended on every visit; if customer declines,
  document it (text the office) so we have a paper trail.
- Take incident-reporting seriously. Document everything in real time.

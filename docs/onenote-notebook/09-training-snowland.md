# Training — PVS SnowLand

Snow work is short-window, high-stress, weather-dependent. Mistakes
happen at 4am in low visibility. Training has to compensate.

---

## Pre-season classroom (October — 1 full day)

All SnowLand crew, returning or new, do this every year before the
first storm.

### Morning — Route + equipment
- Review the season's customer list
- Walk every truck: plow, salt spreader, shovels, brooms, ice
  chippers, headlamps, first-aid
- Hands-on plow attachment + detachment drill (each crew member)
- Practice driveway approach in an empty lot:
  - Reading the lay before pushing
  - Pile location (away from sight lines, never on lawn beds)
  - Garage door stop (24" clear)
  - Mailbox / marker awareness

### Afternoon — Safety, communication, customer expectations
- Storm activation protocol
- Mid-storm pace expectations
- Final-pass standards
- Salt application: light, even, not on plantings
- Incident reporting (slip-and-fall liability awareness)
- Customer call handling — what Office handles, what crew handles

End of day: signed seasonal safety attestation, equipment readiness
sign-off.

---

## First-storm shadow (returning crew + new helpers)

- New helpers ride passenger seat for the first 2 storms
- They watch route execution, learn the customer-by-customer notes
  (this driveway is steep, this one has a tricky turn, this customer
  wants the side walkway done, etc.)
- After 2 storms, they take half the route under crew lead supervision

---

## Plowing technique standards

### Approach
- Slow into the driveway. Read it before you push.
- First pass down the centre, then sides outward.
- Push snow to the side opposite the front door entrance.
- Never push across the road. Never into a neighbour's lawn.
- Watch for: driveway markers, garage door, low shrubs, sprinkler
  heads, paving stone borders.

### Pile placement
- Far corner of the driveway, opposite the door
- Never blocking sight lines at the road
- Never against the garage door
- Never on lawn beds (kills turf in spring — extra liability cost)

### Garage door rule
- Stop the plow 24" short of the garage door
- Shovel the rest by hand — even if it adds 30 seconds
- Plow scratches on paint cost more than the time savings

---

## Walkway clearing standards

- Curb-to-door minimum
- One shovel-width on standard plan, two on premium
- Clear all steps fully — top, treads, sides
- Mailbox: 3-foot box cleared around it (so the carrier can reach)
- Garbage day: clear pickup area

---

## Salt / ice control standards

- Apply lightly. Salt is corrosive and over-application damages
  concrete + kills plants.
- Standard plan: spot-treat icy patches only
- Premium plan: full driveway and walkway light application
- NEVER salt onto:
  - Coloured / stamped concrete (stains)
  - Garden bed perimeters (plant damage)
  - Steel railings (rusts hardware)
- Always wear gloves + eye protection when handling

---

## Driving rules during a storm

- Speed limit on residential routes: 30 km/h max
- All lights on (headlights, plow lights, flashers as appropriate)
- 4WD engaged before entering any driveway
- Phone in dash mount — no handheld dispatch during plow operation
- If visibility drops below 50 m: pull over, wait

## Customer-handling drills

### Customer flags down the truck mid-route
> *"Hi — you're in the route, we'll be at your place within the
> window. If something's urgent, please call the office now and
> they'll prioritise."*

### Customer complains about pile location
> *"Got it — I'll move it on the next pass. Any spot in particular
> that works for you?"*

### Customer asks for off-contract walkway
> *"I can do it today as an add-on — I'll call the office for a
> quote so you know the price before I start. Takes 2 minutes."*

### Customer claims damage
> *"I want to take a look right now and get a photo. Don't worry —
> if we caused it, we own it. Let me call the office and we'll get
> a follow-up scheduled."*

NEVER admit fault on the spot. Document, escalate, let the Owner
handle the resolution.

---

## Storm wrap-up training drill

After every storm, debrief with the new helper:

- What did you see? What's tricky on this route?
- Did anything surprise you?
- Any equipment issue?
- Any customer interaction you want to walk through?

Capture in a notebook. Patterns across drivers become SOP updates.

---

## Cold + fatigue management

Snow shifts can run 12+ hours during heavy storms. Crew leads must:

- Mandate a 15-minute break every 3 hours (heated truck)
- Rotate plow + shovel roles
- Watch for signs of cold stress: shivering that won't stop, slurred
  speech, slow reaction time → END THE SHIFT
- Eat + hydrate every 4 hours — running on coffee alone causes
  mistakes

If a crew member can't continue safely, the Owner is called and a
replacement rotates in. No exceptions.

---

## Sign-off (first storm complete)

After a new helper has run a full storm cycle with the crew lead:

- [ ] Read and understood Vision + Org + General SOPs + SnowLand SOPs
- [ ] Passed pre-season classroom safety attestation
- [ ] Demonstrated safe plow approach and pile placement
- [ ] Demonstrated walkway + salt application standards
- [ ] Successfully handled at least one in-person customer interaction
- [ ] No safety incidents during the shadow phase

Crew lead signs. File copy in HR.

---

## End-of-season debrief (April)

All SnowLand crew, 90 minutes:

- Total storms run, total hours
- Customer feedback themes (good and bad)
- Equipment wins and losses (what's holding up, what to replace)
- Routes that need re-balancing for next year
- Crew rotation into spring divisions

Notes → updates to next October's pre-season classroom.

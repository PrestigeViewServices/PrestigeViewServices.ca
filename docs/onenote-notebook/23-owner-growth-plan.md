# Owner Growth Plan

The business will not outgrow the owner. Investing in yourself isn't
a luxury — it's the highest-leverage spend you have.

---

## The principle

> *"The business is a mirror of the owner. To grow the business,
> grow yourself."*

When you stop learning, the business stops growing. When you burn
out, the team can feel it. When you isolate, blind spots become
liabilities.

Three things to protect:
1. **Your mind** — learning, reading, exposure to new ideas
2. **Your network** — mentors, peers, people who get it
3. **Your body** — sleep, movement, what you eat, how you rest

This isn't soft. It's the foundation.

## Reading plan — one book a month

You don't need to be a constant reader. One book a month is 12 books
a year — enough to be the most-informed contractor in the Valley.

### Core 12 to start (rotate over your first year)

These are the foundational small-business reads. Order them, read
one a month, take notes in this notebook.

1. **The E-Myth Revisited** — Michael Gerber — working ON not IN
2. **Profit First** — Mike Michalowicz — the bank account system
3. **Built to Sell** — John Warrillow — making the business
   independent of you
4. **Traction (EOS)** — Gino Wickman — operating system for small
   business
5. **The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz — leadership
   in the trenches
6. **Drive** — Daniel Pink — what motivates people at work
7. **Atomic Habits** — James Clear — personal operating system
8. **Deep Work** — Cal Newport — focus and output
9. **The Goal** — Eliyahu Goldratt — bottleneck thinking
10. **Never Split the Difference** — Chris Voss — negotiation +
    customer conversations
11. **Crucial Conversations** — Patterson et al. — hard talks with
    team and partners
12. **Essentialism** — Greg McKeown — saying no, doing less

After year one, your reading should branch based on the next
quarter's biggest challenge.

### Reading rules

- Audiobook in the truck counts
- Take ONE action note per chapter — what will I do differently?
- Re-read the best 1-2 books annually
- Quit a book if it isn't working — life's too short

Background learning while you drive, mow, or work out.

### Small business + entrepreneurship
- **How I Built This** (Guy Raz) — origin stories of every kind of
  business

  businesses

applying.

## Mentor + peer network

You need people who've done what you're trying to do, AND people in
the same season as you.

### Find a mentor (someone 5-10 years ahead)

Profile: small-business owner who's grown past where you are now.
Doesn't have to be in your industry.

How to find:
- Ask your accountant / banker / insurance broker for intros
- Local Chamber of Commerce events
- LinkedIn — search local small business owners, send a respectful
  ask
- Customers who are themselves business owners (you'd be surprised
  how many will say yes)

Ask:
> *"I'm growing a small property care business in the Valley.
> I'm trying to {specific challenge}. Would you be open to a
> 30-minute coffee occasionally — I'd value your perspective."*

### Find peers (3-5 owners in your season)

Same-stage, different industries works best — no competition,
maximum learning. A monthly coffee with 3 other small-business
owners is gold.

### Join a peer group (Year 2+)

Formal small-business owner groups:
- Vistage (paid, ~$1,500/mo, very structured)
- EO (Entrepreneurs Organization, paid, peer-driven)
- Local Chamber of Commerce roundtables (free, less rigorous)
- Industry associations (NLPA for landscapers, etc.)

When you're ready, the peer-group fee pays back through better
decisions.

## Physical health

If you go down, the business goes down. This isn't optional.

### Sleep
- Target 7-8 hours a night
- Same wake time every day (yes, weekends too)
- Caffeine cutoff by 2pm
- Phone out of the bedroom

### Movement
- 30 minutes of intentional movement, 5 days/week minimum
- Walk, lift, run, swim, bike — doesn't matter, just do it
- During snow season especially — physical work isn't a substitute
  (it stresses without restoring)

### Food
- Don't grind on coffee + protein bars
- Real meals, sit down for at least one a day
- Hydrate — 2L water minimum, more on hot or snow days

### Annual physical
- Once a year, get the bloodwork done
- Mental health check in (with a counselor or doctor) once a year too

### Sick days
- Take them. The business doesn't end if you're out for 2 days.
- Powering through sick = worse decisions + spreading illness to team

## Mental health + burnout prevention

Small-business owners have one of the highest burnout rates of any
profession. Don't be a statistic.

### Warning signs (catch them early)
- You dread Mondays
- You snap at family for small things
- You can't remember the last time you laughed
- You're drinking more than you used to
- You haven't taken a full day off in 3+ weeks
- You're checking email at 11pm
- You feel like nothing you do is enough

If 3+ apply: stop. Take 3 days off. Talk to your spouse / partner /
a friend. Consider a counselor or coach.

### The off-switch

- Phone notifications: business apps OFF after 7pm
- One day a week phone-free if possible (Sundays?)
- Real vacations — phone in the hotel safe
- A hobby that has NOTHING to do with the business

## Personal finance

A business owner who's broke makes bad decisions. Get your personal
finances under control.

### Targets

- **3-6 months of personal expenses** in a savings account
- **No high-interest personal debt** (credit cards, etc.)
- **RRSP / TFSA contributions** to your limits annually
- **Adequate life + disability insurance** (your business depends
  on you being alive and able-bodied)
- **Will + power of attorney** updated annually

The business and personal finances are connected. A scared owner
makes scared decisions. A solvent owner makes brave ones.

## Family / partner integration

The people closest to you ride this rollercoaster with you.

### Make them part of it
- Weekly business update over dinner — high-level, no overwhelming
  detail
- Celebrate wins together (the company's first $10k month, hiring
  the first crew lead, etc.)
- Be honest about hard months — protect them by being calm, not by
  hiding it

### Protect their time
- Family meals are family meals. Phone face-down.
- Family vacations are real vacations. Email auto-responder ON.
- Date night every week. Non-negotiable.

### Watch for resentment
If your partner is starting to resent the business — listen. Course
correct. The business is supposed to support the family, not consume
it.

## Annual personal review (during your December planning week)

Block 2 hours. Answer honestly:

1. **What am I most proud of this year?** (Personal + professional)
2. **What did I miss this year?** (Family events, friend milestones,
   personal goals)
3. **Am I healthier than a year ago?** (Sleep, weight, energy, mood)
4. **Did I grow this year?** (New skill? New perspective?)
5. **What do I want to be true about my life one year from now?**

Then set 3 personal goals for the year. Equal weight to the business
goals.

---

## The 10-year question

Once a year, ask:

> *"If everything goes well, where do I want to be in 10 years —
> as a person, with my family, with this business?"*

Then ask:

> *"What am I doing today that's getting me there? What am I doing
> today that isn't?"*

Adjust accordingly.

Most owner-operators don't ever ask. They wake up at 55 with a
business that consumed their life. Don't be them.

The notebook you've built is the early version of an answer. Keep
writing it.

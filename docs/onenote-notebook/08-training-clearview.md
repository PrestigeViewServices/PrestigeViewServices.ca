# Training — PVS ClearView

ClearView is the highest-skill, highest-risk division. Window cleaning,
gutter work, pressure washing, soft washing — all customer-visible,
all requiring ladder work. Take training seriously.

---

## Week 1 — Safety + ground-level techniques

### Day 1 — Orientation + ladder safety
- Read Vision + Org Structure + General SOPs + ClearView SOPs
- Ladder safety: 1:4 angle, 3-point contact, 3 rungs above contact
  point, never reach past your belt buckle, tie-off
- Practice setting an extension ladder against the shop wall — 10 reps
  until set-up is automatic
- Sign safety attestation

### Day 2 — Window cleaning fundamentals (interior)
- Spray microfibre, not glass
- Squeegee technique: top-to-bottom, overlapping passes
- Detail edges with dry microfibre
- Streak inspection: stand back 6 feet, raking light
- Practice on the shop windows until streak-free is consistent

### Day 3 — Exterior windows, ground floor only
- Same technique outside, ground level only
- Pre-rinse heavily soiled windows
- Water-fed pole intro (no climbing yet)
- Customer-handling drill: greeting at the door

### Day 4 — Gutter basics
- On the ground: tarp set-up, debris bagging
- Hose flush technique: top down, confirm flow at outlet
- Inspection checklist: fasteners, sag, sealant, rust
- Practice on the shop building with crew lead spotting

### Day 5 — Ladder day
- Step ladder work on first-floor windows + low gutters
- Extension ladder under supervision only
- Crew lead spots every climb
- End-of-week ladder safety quiz

## Streak-free guarantee — the brand promise

This is the most important quality standard in ClearView. Drill it in.

**What counts as a streak:**
- Visible line, smudge, or dried-water mark on the glass
- Edge film where the squeegee didn't reach
- Sill water marks the customer can see

**How to never leave one:**
1. Clean glass first, sill last
2. Squeegee in overlapping passes (overlap 1 inch)
3. Wipe the squeegee blade after every pass
4. Detail the edges with a dry, clean microfibre
5. Final check: walk 6 feet back, look from multiple angles, ESPECIALLY
   the angle the customer looks from (often the kitchen sink or
   living room couch)

**If you spot a streak after-the-fact:** go back, do it again,
no charge. The guarantee is the brand.

## Pressure vs. soft wash — when each is appropriate

| Surface | Method | Pressure | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Concrete driveway | Pressure + surface cleaner | 2500–3000 PSI | Test patch always |
| Pavers | Pressure + surface cleaner | 2000 PSI | Quote re-sanding |
| Brick | Medium pressure | 1500–2000 PSI | Watch for old mortar |
| Wood deck | Soft wash or low pressure | ≤ 1200 PSI | With grain only |
| Vinyl / aluminum siding | Soft wash | ≤ 500 PSI | Bottom-up apply, top-down rinse |
| Soffit / fascia | Soft wash | ≤ 500 PSI | Watch wind drift |
| Stucco | Soft wash only | ≤ 300 PSI | Test patch, no high pressure ever |

When in doubt — soft wash. You can always go up in pressure; you
can't undo damage.

## Ladder + height safety quiz (must pass before independent height work)

1. What angle is a safe ladder placement?
   *(1 foot out for every 4 feet up)*
2. How far above the contact point should the ladder extend?
   *(3 rungs)*
3. What's the maximum distance you can reach to either side without
   moving the ladder?
   *(Belt buckle should never cross outside the rails)*
4. When is solo ladder work allowed?
   *(Never. ClearView ladder work requires a spotter.)*
5. What weather conditions stop ladder work immediately?
   *(Wind > 25 km/h, rain, ice, lightning visible)*
6. What's the clearance distance from any overhead power line?
   *(10 feet — assume all lines are live)*

Crew lead administers verbally on Day 5. Must score 100% to advance.

## Customer-handling drills

### Arrival
> *"Good morning, I'm {name} from Prestige View Services — we're here
> for your window cleaning. I'm going to walk the property quickly to
> get my bearings — anything you'd like me to know before I start?"*

### Mid-job customer check
*(If the customer's home and the job is long)*
> *"Just wanted to let you know we're about halfway — the upper floor
> is next. Should take another 45 minutes."*

### Streak callback
> *"Thanks for letting me know — I'll come right back. Streak-free is
> our guarantee, no charge to make it right."*

### Spot a problem on the home (rotted fascia, etc.)
> *"While I was up there I noticed {issue}. I'll have the office send
> you a quote so you can decide — it's not urgent but worth knowing."*

### Customer wants a discount
> *"Our pricing's the same for every customer in your neighbourhood —
> we don't change quotes after a job. What I can do is bundle this
> with your next gutter clean to save on the trip. Want me to ask
> the office to put together a bundle quote?"*

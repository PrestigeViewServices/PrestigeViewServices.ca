# Training — PVS LawnPros

A new helper or crew lead should be able to run a full LawnPros visit
solo by the end of week 2. This curriculum gets them there.

---

## Week 1 — Shadow + safety

### Day 1 — Orientation
- Read Vision + Org Structure + General SOPs + LawnPros SOPs
- Tour the shop / equipment area
- Watch equipment startup / shutdown for every machine (mowers,
  trimmer, blower, edger, aerator)
- Sign safety attestation: "I understand the safety rules in the
  LawnPros SOPs"
- Issue branded shirt + cap

### Day 2 — Shadow ride-along
- Ride with a crew lead the full day
- Observe: route order, equipment loading, customer arrival, work
  flow, photo capture, end-of-day cleanup
- Don't touch equipment yet — just watch

### Day 3 — Trimmer + blower
- Spend the day operating ONLY the string trimmer and backpack blower
- Crew lead supervises every property
- Goal: clean, straight trim lines; full blow-down of walkways

### Day 4 — Mower introduction
- Empty-lot drills on a residential or commercial lawn at low speed
- Practice: starting, stopping, turns, mower lift on slopes
- Don't run on a customer property unless the crew lead is right
  behind

### Day 5 — Full visit with supervision
- Run two full visits start-to-finish (mow, trim, edge, blow)
- Crew lead inspects every property before leaving
- Photo handoff demonstration

---

## Week 2 — Independent work + quality

### Days 6–10
- Helper runs the smaller properties on the route independently
- Crew lead does QC walk on every job for the first 3 days
- Day 8: introduce the aerator. Same shadow → drill → live progression.
- Day 10: 30-minute end-of-week debrief — what's clicking, what isn't

---

## Quality standards (memorize)

Every visit, before you leave, look for:

1. **Stripe pattern** — visible from the street, alternating direction
   each visit
2. **Crisp edges** — string-trim and hard-edge lines should be straight
   and consistent depth
3. **No scalped spots** — turns near beds, trees, or grade changes
4. **No clippings on walkways, drive, patio** — blow it all back
5. **Beds clean** — no clippings blown into mulch
6. **Gates closed and latched** — for dog safety
7. **No equipment marks** — tire ruts, scorched grass from idle

---

## Equipment care training

Every helper learns BEFORE going on solo runs:

- **Mower blade sharpening** — basic angle, balance check, when to
  replace
- **String trimmer line replacement** — load the head, tension test
- **2-stroke fuel mixing** — correct ratio, fresh stock only, label
  every can
- **Air filter check** — visual inspection daily, replace per spec
- **Belt + cable inspection** — weekly check, flag wear to crew lead
- **Daily wash-down** — undercarriage of riding mower after every shift

## Customer-handling drills (practice with crew lead)

### Greeting at the door
> *"Hi, I'm {name} from Prestige View Services — we're here for
> your {service} today. Anything you'd like me to take a look at
> before we start?"*

### When asked for an add-on quote
> *"Happy to take a look — let me snap a photo and have the office
> send you a quote today. They'll know the right price."*

### When asked about a different service
> *"Yes, our {division} crew handles that. Want me to have the
> office call you with an estimate?"*

### When the customer points out a problem
> *"Got it — I'll take a look. If it's something I can fix in the
> next 10 minutes, we'll handle it today. Anything bigger, the
> office sends a quote so you can decide."*

### When the customer is upset
> *"I hear you. Let me take a look right now, and I'll loop in
> the office so we can make this right. Thank you for telling me
> in person — that's the only way we get better."*

Never argue. Never make excuses. Listen, document, escalate.

## Common mistakes to coach out

| Mistake | Why it matters | Coach |
|---|---|---|
| Same mowing direction every visit | Compacts the turf, no stripe pattern | Alternate direction every visit |
| Trimming into garden beds | Damages plants, looks careless | Edge to the bed border only |
| Speeding through turns | Scalps the lawn near beds and trees | Slow at every turn — half-second pause |
| Blowing clippings into beds | Looks like a shortcut | Blow them back onto the lawn or into the bag |
| Skipping the post-visit photo | We lose proof of quality | Photo before you load up |
| Leaving without checking the gate | Dog escapes, customer loses trust | Crew lead's checklist last step |

## Sign-off

By end of Week 2, the helper signs off on:

- [ ] Read and understood Vision + Org + General SOPs + LawnPros SOPs
- [ ] Demonstrated safe operation of every LawnPros machine
- [ ] Ran 5 properties solo to quality standard
- [ ] Captured before/after photos for every job
- [ ] Completed first customer add-on referral (documented + sent to
      office)

Crew lead signs the other side. File copy in HR folder.

---

## 30-day check-in

Owner or Operations sits with the new helper for 20 minutes:

- What's working? What's frustrating?
- What customers do they remember? Why?
- Where do they want to be in 12 months? Crew lead path?
- Any safety concern they haven't flagged?
- Any equipment gripe?

Notes captured, follow-ups assigned.

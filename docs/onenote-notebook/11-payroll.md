# Payroll

How PVS pays the team — process, schedule, tools, compliance.

This isn't tax or HR advice — confirm specifics with your accountant
and the CRA before changing pay structure. The procedures below are
operational, not legal.

---

## Pay structure (default)

### Crew Helper
- Hourly rate at provincial minimum + skill premium
- Reviewed at 30 days, 90 days, and annually
- Overtime: 1.5× after 44 hours/week per Ontario ESA
- Reliable hours: minimum 30/week guaranteed during in-season
  (May–Oct for lawn/exterior, Nov–Apr for snow)

### Crew Lead
- Higher hourly rate reflecting responsibility + skill
- Eligible for monthly quality bonus (see below)
- Annual review at start of fiscal year
- Phone allowance: $40/month for personal phone use on the job

### Office / Dispatch
- Hourly or salaried — depends on the hire
- Standard 40-hour week
- Eligible for quarterly bonus tied to customer-retention metric

### Operations Manager
- Salaried
- Eligible for quarterly profit-share

### Owner / Operator
- Owner's draw, set quarterly based on cash position and tax planning
- Talk to the accountant before any change

## Quality bonus (Crew Lead)

Paid monthly on the next payroll after month end.

Threshold | Bonus
---|---
Zero complaints + 4+ Google reviews collected for the month | $150
Zero complaints, fewer than 4 reviews | $75
1+ complaint (resolved by Crew Lead within authority) | $0
1+ complaint (escalated, partially the crew's fault) | $0, written feedback to Crew Lead
1+ complaint (clearly the crew's fault) | Coaching session, no bonus, performance plan if recurring

## Pay schedule

- **Pay period:** bi-weekly (every two weeks, Sunday–Saturday)
- **Pay date:** Friday following the close of the pay period (5 business days)
- **Deposit:** direct deposit only. New hires bring a void cheque on Day 1.
- **First cheque:** usually within 7–14 days of start date, depending on
  where in the pay cycle the hire lands

---

## Time tracking

- Crew lead logs start and end times for each crew member daily in
  the dispatch tool
- Helper confirms hours by end-of-week text to the crew lead
- Discrepancies flagged within 48 hours — same-pay-period adjustments
  only
- Travel time between properties is paid time; commute from home to
  first job and last job to home is unpaid

## Tools

Pick ONE payroll provider and stick with it. Recommended for small
Canadian businesses (talk to the accountant before final pick):

- **Wagepoint** — Canadian, designed for small biz, friendly UI
- **Wave Payroll** — affordable, integrates with Wave accounting
- **QuickBooks Payroll** — works well if accounting is already in QBO
- **Knit People** — Canadian, strong HR features included

Whichever is picked:
- Set up direct deposit + CRA remittance auto-debit
- Configure provincial tax tables for Ontario
- Enable T4 generation at year-end (auto-handled by all of the above)
- Enable ROE generation for departures

---

## CRA remittance

- Source deductions (CPP, EI, income tax) remitted monthly or
  quarterly depending on your remitter status
- Payroll provider handles the calculation + filing
- Owner reviews and approves the remittance every period
- Save every CRA receipt — file in the year-end folder

---

## Year-end (December → February)

1. **Dec 31** — Last pay period of the year closes
2. **Jan 1–15** — Reconcile all payroll totals with bank
3. **Jan 16–31** — Generate T4s in the payroll system
4. **Feb 28** — T4 distribution deadline (provider sends to employees;
   you confirm receipt)
5. **Feb 28** — T4 summary filing deadline to CRA (provider files;
   you confirm submission)
6. **Mar 1** — Year folder closed, archive copy kept 7 years

## ROE (Record of Employment)

Issued whenever an employee:
- Quits
- Is laid off
- Is fired
- Takes parental / sick leave > 7 days

Must be filed with Service Canada within 5 calendar days of the
last day of work. Payroll provider does this electronically — the
trigger is on the Owner.

## Subcontractors / 1099 work

If you ever bring on a subcontractor (e.g. for tree work outside
your insurance scope, or a one-off paint touch-up):

- Subcontractor invoices YOU; no payroll involved
- Confirm they have their own insurance + WSIB before any work
- Issue them a T4A in February for total payments made in the
  prior calendar year
- Keep their insurance certificates on file

---

## Cash handling

- No cash payments from customers, ever. E-transfer / cheque / card
  only.
- If a customer insists on cash, politely decline and offer e-transfer.
  This protects the business from tax exposure and the crew from
  liability.

---

## Compliance reminders

- Post ESA poster + Health & Safety policy in any office or shop
- WSIB account active and premiums paid quarterly
- Insurance certificate updated annually + on file for customer requests
- Driver's abstract on file for everyone who operates a company vehicle
- Annual safety attestation signed by every crew member
- Annual review meeting documented for every salaried role

---

## Common payroll mistakes to avoid

1. Paying cash off the books — small short-term win, large long-term
   tax + WSIB exposure
2. Misclassifying employees as contractors — CRA will reclassify and
   penalise
3. Skipping overtime — Ontario ESA is non-negotiable; OT at 1.5× kicks
   in at 44 hours
4. Forgetting WSIB — required for most employers from day one
5. Late T4 distribution — penalty per employee per day late

# Marketing Playbook

How new customers find PVS — every channel, every cadence, with
honest priority. This is the owner's strategic view. The hands-on
posting work lives in `13-social-media.md`.

---

## The marketing principle

You don't need to be everywhere. You need to be **findable when
someone in the Valley needs property care**.

That means **two things matter most**, and the rest is support:

1. **Local search** (Google + Maps) — when someone Googles "lawn
   care Petawawa," you show up
2. **Referrals + reviews** — when a neighbour asks who does their
   gutters, your name comes up

Everything else (social, paid ads, flyers) feeds these two. Get them
right first; layer the rest after.

## #1 Local search — Google Business Profile

The GBP listing is your most valuable marketing asset. More than the
website. More than social.

### Weekly GBP tasks (Office or you — 30 min Monday)

- [ ] Post 1 update (use templates from `13-social-media.md`)
- [ ] Add 2–4 new photos (jobs done last week)
- [ ] Respond to every new review (positive or negative)
- [ ] Answer any new Q&A
- [ ] Check Insights — searches, calls, direction requests

### Monthly GBP tasks (you — 30 min)

- [ ] Update services list if anything changed
- [ ] Update business hours if seasonal
- [ ] Review top search terms — what are people finding you for?
- [ ] Set one experimental post type to try this month

### The GBP review-collection target

**2 reviews per week minimum.** That's 100+ per year. Every crew
lead is trained to ask at job completion (`10-scripts-customer.md`).
Office sends the review link by text within 2 hours of job close.

A business with 200 reviews and a 4.8 average outranks a business
with 30 reviews and a 5.0 — almost every time. Volume + recency win.

## #1 Reviews — the system

### Collection (in operations)
- Crew lead asks at job close
- Office sends review link by text within 2 hours
- Auto-follow-up text at 7 days if no review left
- Stop after one follow-up — never pester

### Response (in operations — Office or you)
- Within 24 hours, every review, public response
- Positive: thank by first name, mention the service done
- Negative: empathy first, invite offline conversation, no defense

### Reviews → marketing
- Best reviews get screenshot + reposted (with first name + town
  only) on social
- Best 5 reviews of the year displayed on the website (already
  wired up at `/reviews`)
- Annual reviews report — track avg star rating quarterly

### Negative review recovery (the most important play)

A handled negative review can convert into a 5-star update. The
process:

1. Public response within 24 hours (calm, professional)
2. Phone call from owner within 48 hours
3. Make it right — re-do work, refund, credit, whatever fits
4. Two weeks later, ASK: *"If you feel we made it right, would you
   consider updating the review?"*
5. Don't push if they say no — but most who feel heard will update

## #2 Referrals — the formal program

Word-of-mouth happens whether you plan for it or not. A FORMAL
referral program 3-5x what happens organically.

### The PVS referral offer

For every customer who refers a friend who becomes a paying
customer:
- **$50 credit** on the referrer's next service
- **$25 credit** on the new customer's first service
- Sent automatically after the first invoice is paid

### How to promote it
- Every invoice footer: "Refer a friend — both of you save. Ask us how."
- Annual postcard (December): "Thanks for being a customer. Here's
  your referral link."
- Crew lead handoff: "If you know anyone else who needs us, ask the
  office about our referral credit — both of you get $25-50 off."
- Door hangers at completed-job neighbours: "We just finished a job
  at {street name} — want a quote for yours?"

### Track it
- Office tags every new lead with referral source
- Monthly: count referrals, total referral credits issued, conversion
  rate
- Goal: 25% of new customers come from referrals within 2 years

Ottawa Valley demographics live on Facebook. Use it.

Join + engage in:
- "Petawawa Buy & Sell"

- Any town-specific local group
- "Ottawa Valley Home Owners"

  you've been a contributor — don't self-promote

## #3 Door hangers + yard signs

Cheap, hyperlocal, work especially well in dense subdivisions.

### Door hanger campaign
- Print 500 at a time (Vistaprint or local printer)
- After every job, hang on the 5 closest neighbours' doors
- Include: photo of recent work, "We just finished {service} at
  {street}", phone + quote URL
- Track conversion: which subdivisions convert best?

### Yard signs
- "Job by PVS" sign at every completed job, with permission
- Leave for 7-14 days, pick up on next route through
- Best ROI: large suburban lawns where the sign is visible from the
  road

## #4 Google Ads (paid search) — only after organic is humming

Skip Google Ads until:
- GBP is dialled in
- You have 50+ reviews
- Organic search is bringing in 10+ leads/week
- You have the budget to commit $500-1500/month for 6+ months

When you're ready:

### Setup (one-time, 4 hours with a contractor)
- One campaign per division (LawnPros, ClearView, SnowLand)
- Geo-targeted to your service areas only — don't waste impressions
- Negative keywords: "free," "DIY," "how to," "career," "jobs"
- Match types: phrase + exact only (no broad)
- Bid strategy: maximize conversions
- Conversion tracking: phone call + quote form submission

### Monthly review (1 hour)
- Cost per lead by campaign
- Quality score per ad group
- Search terms report — add negatives, kill underperformers
- Pause anything > $50 cost per lead

**Honest take:** for residential property care in a small market,
organic + referrals usually outperform paid. Test small, scale only
if the unit economics work.

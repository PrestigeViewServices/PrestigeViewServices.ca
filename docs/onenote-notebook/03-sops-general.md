# SOPs — Company-Wide

These SOPs apply to every division. Division-specific procedures are in
the next three files.

---

## Customer intake

**Trigger:** Inbound call, website form, email, or referral.

1. Within 5 minutes of intake, create a Lead record with:
   - Name, phone, email
   - Property address (full)
   - Service(s) requested
   - How they heard about us (record the channel — Google, referral,
     drive-by, etc.)
2. If urgent (snow callout, water-flow emergency from gutters), call
   back within 30 minutes during business hours.
3. If routine, send a quote email within one business day.
4. Schedule a follow-up call if no response in 3 business days.
5. Mark the Lead as Won, Lost, or Nurture after the customer's decision.
   Track Lost-reason — it's free product feedback.

**Standard:** Free quote, returned within one business day, 95% of the time.

## Quoting

**Trigger:** New lead requesting service.

1. Pull the property on Google Earth / Street View. Sanity-check the
   size of the job before quoting.
2. Use the standard pricing sheet (kept in Office, updated quarterly).
3. For first-time customers, quote with:
   - Per-visit price
   - Recurring discount (5–10% off if they sign a season)
   - Bundle discount (if multiple services)
4. Send the quote by email with a Get-Quote-Approved link.
   Include:
   - Detailed scope of work
   - Price
   - Expected duration
   - Earliest available start
   - Insurance certificate attached
5. Follow up by phone 2 business days later if no response.
6. Once accepted, schedule and confirm.

## Dispatch (daily routing)

**Trigger:** End of business day, prepare the next day's runs.

1. Pull the next day's bookings from the scheduling tool.
2. Group jobs by geography (one crew = one neighbourhood per half-day
   where possible — saves drive time).
3. Print each crew lead a route sheet with:
   - Customer name, address, gate code if applicable
   - Service to be performed
   - Special notes (dog on premises, side gate, etc.)
   - Equipment list for the day
4. Confirm by 8:00pm with the crew lead by text.
5. First-time customers get a confirmation text the morning of:
   *"Hi {name}, the PVS crew is on the way for {service} today —
   ETA {window}. Reply STOP to opt out of these messages."*

## On-site arrival & handoff

**Crew lead** does this at every job:

1. Park out of the customer's way. Branded truck visible from street.
2. Knock or ring at the front door before starting outdoor work, in
   case the customer wants to flag anything.
3. Confirm the scope verbally if the customer is present. If not, the
   route sheet is the source of truth — do NOT take verbal add-ons
   from a child or houseguest without dispatch approval.
4. Take a Before photo of the work area.
5. Do the work.
6. Take an After photo.
7. Blow / sweep the work area. Walk it. Leave it cleaner than found.
8. If the customer is home, do a 30-second handoff:
   *"All done. The {area} is back open. Anything you noticed you'd
   like us to flag for next time?"*
9. If the customer is not home, leave a door hanger with date, crew
   name, and a "satisfaction guaranteed — call within 48 hours" note.
10. Mark the job Complete in the dispatch tool with a one-line note +
    upload photos.

## Billing & invoicing

**Trigger:** Job marked Complete.

1. Invoice goes out **same day** for one-off jobs, **end of week** for
   recurring routes.
2. Standard payment terms: due on receipt for one-off, net-15 for
   recurring contracts.
3. Accepted methods: e-transfer (preferred), credit card, cheque.
4. Auto-reminder at 7 days overdue, personal call from Office at
   14 days overdue.
5. At 30 days overdue, pause future service and notify the customer in
   writing. Owner approves any exceptions.

## Customer complaints

**Trigger:** Any negative feedback — call, email, online review, in
person to a crew member.

1. Acknowledge within 4 business hours. Same day if it's a Google review.
2. Listen first. Don't defend, don't deflect. Take notes.
3. Ask: *"What would make this right for you?"*
4. Standard remedies (Crew Lead can offer up to $200 in credit or
   re-work; Owner approves more):
   - Re-do the work at no charge
   - Credit on next visit
   - Refund (last resort — only when re-work isn't possible)
5. Log the complaint in the Support table with status + resolution.
6. Owner reviews complaint log weekly. Patterns get pulled into
   training or SOP updates.
7. If the customer leaves a public review, respond publicly within
   24 hours — professional, empathetic, no blame.

## Review collection

**Trigger:** Job marked Complete + customer expressed satisfaction.

1. End-of-visit handoff: *"If we did a good job today, the best way
   to thank us is to leave a quick Google review. I'll send you the
   link — takes 30 seconds."*
2. Office sends the review link by text within 24 hours of the job.
3. Reviews tracked in admin dashboard.
4. Negative reviews handled per Complaints SOP above.

**Standard:** 1 review collected per 4 completed jobs minimum. Goal
is 1 per 2.

---

## Safety incidents

**Trigger:** Any injury, near-miss, property damage, or vehicle incident.

1. Make the scene safe. Call 911 if injuries are anything more than
   minor.
2. Crew lead calls Owner within 30 minutes, regardless of severity.
3. Take photos of: the scene, any damage, equipment involved.
4. Get contact info of any witnesses.
5. Within 24 hours, file a written report (template in Office):
   - Date, time, location
   - Who was involved
   - What happened (factual, no opinion)
   - Photos attached
   - What we're doing to prevent recurrence
6. Owner files insurance claim if property damage exists.
7. Incident logged in the safety log (kept in Office). Reviewed
   monthly.

# Inventory

What we own, what we use, where we buy it, when we re-order.

Every line item has a name, a supplier, a current quantity, a re-order
threshold, and a re-order quantity. Keep this list updated — the
penalty for being out of trimmer line at 7am Monday is real.

---

## How to use this section

- The tables below are the **current state** snapshot
- Update quantities after every supply run (paper or app — pick one
  and stick)
- Recommended app: **Sortly** (free tier covers up to 100 entries)
  OR a simple Google Sheet
- Re-order threshold = minimum quantity that triggers a buy
- Re-order quantity = how much to buy when below threshold

## Monthly inventory check (Office or Operations)

1st of every month, 30 minutes:
- Walk the shop / storage with this checklist
- Update quantities
- Order anything below re-order threshold
- Note unusual usage (one bag of salt gone in 2 days means we plowed
  a lot OR someone's overusing — investigate)

---

## Annual inventory audit (Owner or accountant)

End of fiscal year:
- Photograph + count every capital asset
- Match to asset list for insurance
- Note any equipment to retire / replace next year
- Update depreciation schedule with accountant
- Update insurance schedule with broker

---

## Loss / damage protocol

- Equipment lost or damaged on a job: crew lead photographs + reports
  same day
- Anything > $250 to replace: Owner approves the replacement
- Persistent loss with one person: addressed in performance review
- Theft: police report filed + insurance claim

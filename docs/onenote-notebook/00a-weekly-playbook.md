# Owner's Weekly Playbook — Print + Pin Above Desk

The condensed reference. If you only ever read one file in this
notebook on a regular basis, make it this one. Print it. Laminate it.
Pin it where you sit in the morning.

---

## The one filter for every owner decision

> **Does this make us more reliable, more recognisable, or more
> recurring? If not, why are we doing it?**

If the answer isn't immediate — the answer is no.

---

## Daily rhythm (30 min total)

### 6:30am — Morning numbers (15 min)
- Open the dashboard
- Yesterday's revenue
- Today's scheduled jobs (count + total $)
- Cash position
- Read overnight messages; flag escalations
- Send any blocker-removing texts
- **Don't open the laptop. Don't start tasks.**

### End of workday — Evening wrap (15 min)
- Did every scheduled job get done? If no, why?
- Any customer complaints today?
- Tomorrow confirmed (route built + crews briefed)?
- One sentence in the owner journal

## Monthly rhythm — Last Friday (4 hours)

1. **Numbers close** (1 hr) — P&L vs. plan, 12-month rolling
2. **Team check-in** (45 min) — wins, numbers, one improvement
3. **Marketing review** (1 hr) — channels, leads, reviews, what to
   double down on
4. **Personal review** (45 min) — what only I could do, sleep, energy,
   blocked strategic time?
5. **Update the notebook** (30 min) — improve one section

---

## Quarterly rhythm — first Friday of new quarter (half day)

1. **Quarter review** (90 min) — score rocks, what changed
2. **Quarter plan** (90 min) — set 3 new rocks (build a system,
   grow revenue, improve the team)
3. **Comp + bonus check** (30 min) — anyone undercompensated?

---

## Annual rhythm — full week in December

- Day 1: Look back — score every rock from the year
- Day 2: Numbers — full P&L, ratios, top 20% customers
- Day 3: Team — every seat, hire plan, compensation
- Day 4: Market — competitors, customer interviews, trends
- Day 5: Next year — vision, revenue target, quarterly rocks
- Day 6: Communicate — one-page vision to team, schedule kickoff
- Day 7: Rest

## The 12 numbers — KPI scorecard

### Weekly (Friday 4pm, 10 min)
1. Revenue this week vs. plan
2. Jobs completed vs. capacity
3. New leads + source
4. Quotes sent + close rate (target 50-65%)
5. Cash position (target 1+ month opex)

### Monthly (last Friday, 30 min)
6. New customers this month
7. Churn / pause rate (target < 5% monthly)
8. Average customer LTV (target $800+ Y1)
9. Customer acquisition cost (target < $50 Y1)
10. Google reviews + average rating (2/week + 4.8+)
11. Recurring revenue % (target 50% Y1, 80% Y3)
12. Net profit margin (target 10-15% Y1, 20-25% Y3)

**Color-code green / yellow / red.** Act on reds at 2-week mark.

## The four leverage points — owner time goes HERE

When you have an hour of owner time, spend it on one of:

1. **Hire the next person well** — one great hire = 20+ hr/wk freed
2. **Pricing change** — 5% increase often goes entirely to bottom line
3. **Marketing system that runs without you** — referrals + GBP +
   recurring contracts compound
4. **Clear standards so the team self-corrects** — every SOP is leverage

If your hour isn't on one of these, ask why.

## Delegation framework (when a task lands)

Ask in this order:
1. Should this exist at all? (kill if not)
2. Can a system / automation do it?
3. Can it be outsourced?
4. Can a team member do it (after I document the SOP)?
5. Does this need ME?

Only step 5 deserves your time.

### 5-step delegation script
1. "Here's what needs to happen and why it matters."
2. "Here's how I do it today — watch me once, then I'll watch you."
3. "Here's where it lives — the file, the SOP, the checklist."
4. "Here's how I'll know it's working — the metric + check-in cadence."
5. "From today, this is yours. Bring me the metric, not the work."

## Owner anti-patterns — catch and fix one per week

- 🚩 Answering routine customer calls during business hours
- 🚩 No full day off in a month
- 🚩 Can't see last week's revenue without opening 3 tools
- 🚩 Dispatch board breaks if you're gone 5 days
- 🚩 Haven't updated this notebook in over a month
- 🚩 Starting jobs alongside crew "to help out"
- 🚩 Pricing decisions case-by-case instead of by policy
- 🚩 Reactive hiring (panic-hire) instead of proactive (warm pipeline)

## The annual decision letter

Every December, write a one-page letter to your future self dated for
next December. Include:
- Where I think the business will be one year from now
- What I'm worried about
- What I'm most excited about
- One thing I'm committing to next year, no exceptions

Seal it. Open next December.
After 5 years you have a journal more valuable than any book in the
notebook.

---

## When stuck — the three questions

1. **Am I the only person who can do this?** If yes — do I love it, or
   haven't I hired / trained / documented it yet?
2. **Will this matter in 3 months?** If no — close it, move on.
3. **What would the version of me 5 years from now want me to do
   today?** That's the answer.

# Owner Operating System

Your weekly heartbeat. If you do nothing else from this notebook, do
the rhythms below — they're the engine.

---

## Daily — 30 minutes total, before crews leave

### Morning (15 min, 6:30am)

- [ ] Open the dashboard (see `18-kpis-scorecard.md`)
  - Yesterday's revenue
  - Today's scheduled jobs (count + total expected $)
  - Cash position
- [ ] Read overnight emails / texts — flag any escalation
- [ ] Send any blocker-removing texts (e.g. "approved, go ahead")
- [ ] Eat. Move. Don't open the laptop yet.

### Evening (15 min, end of workday)

- [ ] Did every scheduled job get done? If no, why?
- [ ] Any customer complaints today? Resolved or escalated?
- [ ] Tomorrow's confirmed (route built, crews briefed)?
- [ ] One sentence in your owner journal: "Today, the company…"

**You do NOT do during the day:** chase invoices, dispatch routes,
answer routine customer calls, run errands for the crew. If you find
yourself doing these, ask: who SHOULD be doing this, and what's
stopping me from handing it off?

---

## Weekly — 2 hours total, Monday + Friday

### Monday morning huddle (15 min, 7:00am)

- All-hands (in person if possible, else group video)
- 3 questions:
  1. What's the week look like? (Office: route count + customer flags)
  2. Anything broken from last week? (Crew leads)
  3. Who needs help from someone else in the room? (Anyone)
- That's it. No reports, no slides. Get the team moving.

### Wednesday lunch — Office sync (30 min, 12:00pm)

Just you + the Office / Dispatch person.

- Lead pipeline: new this week, quoted this week, won, lost (with
  reasons)
- Cash: invoices sent, invoices paid, anything > 14 days overdue
- Any customer flags I need to know about
- Anything they need from me to do their job better

### Friday wrap (45 min, 4:00pm)

The most important meeting of your week.

#### KPI scorecard (15 min) — alone, with coffee

- Open the scorecard (12 numbers, all in one spreadsheet)
- Update every cell from this week's data
- Mark each as Green / Yellow / Red vs. target
- Write a one-line note on each Red

#### Crew lead debrief (15 min) — group

- 1 win
- 1 thing that broke
- 1 thing to fix next week
- Action items assigned with owners + due dates

#### Plan next week (15 min) — alone

- Block 1 morning for one Leverage activity (hiring, pricing,
  marketing system, documentation)
- Block 1 evening for review of one Operator Track SOP
- Confirm Monday huddle agenda

## Monthly — 4 hours total, last Friday of the month

### Numbers close (1 hour)

- Pull P&L for the month from the bookkeeping system
- Compare to plan
- Update the 12-month rolling spreadsheet
- Flag anything > 10% variance from plan

### Team check-in (45 min)

Full team, in person.
- Last month wins (specific, named)
- Last month numbers (revenue, jobs done, reviews collected)
- One company-wide improvement we're committing to next month
- Birthdays, anniversaries, anything personal worth recognizing

### Marketing review (1 hour)

Open `15-marketing-playbook.md`. Honest audit:

- Posts published vs. planned
- New leads by channel
- Cost per lead (paid channels only)
- New reviews collected
- GBP photo updates done
- Anything that crushed it last month — do more
- Anything that flopped — kill or fix

### Personal review (45 min)

- What did I work on that ONLY I could do? (Should be most of my
  hours.)
- What did I do that someone else should be doing? (Plan the
  handoff.)
- Did I block strategic time this month? Or did fires win?
- How am I sleeping, eating, moving? (Owner burnout is a real risk.)

### Update the notebook (30 min)

Pick one section. Re-read it. Improve one thing. Commit the change.

## Quarterly — half a day, first Friday of new quarter

The 90-day review + reset.

### Quarter review (90 min)

- Were the 3 quarterly rocks accomplished? (See `19-strategic-planning.md`)
- What changed in the market this quarter?
- What changed about competitors?
- What changed about the team?
- Big customer wins? Losses? Pattern?

### Quarter plan (90 min)

- Set 3 rocks for the next 90 days. No more.
- Each rock has: owner, success metric, weekly check-in slot
- One rock should be "build a system" (move you further out of
  ops)
- One rock should be "grow revenue" (hire, expand, raise prices)
- One rock should be "improve the team" (training, culture,
  retention)

### Comp + bonus check (30 min)

- Are crew leads making their bonus thresholds?
- Are office staff hitting their quarterly metrics?
- Anyone undercompensated relative to value? Fix before they leave.

## Annually — one full week off, slow season (December)

The big one. Block it like a sacred holiday.

### Numbers review (full day)

- Full P&L for the year
- Compare to plan
- Compute key ratios: revenue per crew, profit per division, top 20%
  customers by revenue, top complaint reasons
- Set NEXT year's revenue target + division mix

### Vision update (half day)

- Re-read `01-vision.md`
- Are the 1/3/5-year goals still right?
- Anything you've outgrown? Anything new?
- Edit the file, commit the change

### Team review (full day)

- Each person: in the right seat? promoted? exited?
- Hire plan for next year — what roles, when, what budget
- Any hard conversations you've been avoiding — schedule them

### Marketing + brand review (half day)

- Top 5 sources of new customers this year — double down
- Any channels that flopped — kill them
- Brand assets stale? Update them
- Website needs anything? Note for the dev backlog

### Strategic plan for the year (full day)

- 4 quarterly rocks for the year (one per quarter)
- Annual revenue target broken into quarterly targets
- Capital expenditures budget (equipment, vehicles, marketing)
- Cash flow projection for the year

### Personal year ahead (half day)

- Books to read (see `23-owner-growth-plan.md`)
- One conference / workshop to attend
- One mentor to nurture relationship with
- One vacation scheduled — actually book it now

## Delegation framework

When something lands on your plate, ask in order:

1. **Should this exist at all?** (Many tasks are leftover from a
   previous reality. Kill what doesn't matter.)
2. **Can a system do it?** (Auto-reminder, scheduler, template)
3. **Can it be outsourced?** (Bookkeeping, social media, lawn care
   for your own house — all candidates)
4. **Can a team member do it?** (Document the SOP, train once, hand
   off)
5. **Does it need YOU?** (Pricing decisions, hiring decisions, big
   customer relationships — yes. Everything else — no.)

### The 5-step delegation script (use this verbatim)

> 1. *"Here's what needs to happen and why it matters."*
> 2. *"Here's how I do it today — watch me once, then I'll watch
>     you do it."*
> 3. *"Here's where it lives — the file, the SOP, the checklist."*
> 4. *"Here's how I'll know it's working — the metric and the
>     check-in cadence."*
> 5. *"From today forward, this is yours. Bring me the metric, not
>     the work. I trust you."*

## Owner anti-patterns (catch yourself)

- 🚩 You're answering routine customer calls during business hours
- 🚩 You haven't taken a full day off in a month
- 🚩 You don't know last week's revenue without opening 3 tools
- 🚩 The dispatch board would break if you stepped away for 5 days
- 🚩 You haven't updated this notebook in over a month
- 🚩 You're starting jobs alongside the crew "to help out"
- 🚩 You're making pricing decisions case-by-case instead of by
  policy
- 🚩 Hiring is reactive (someone quit, panic-hire) instead of
  proactive (next role is identified, candidate pipeline warm)

Catching any of these is good. Fix one per week.

## The owner's promise

You owe your team:
- A clear direction (vision)
- A fair shake (good pay, good schedule, growth path)
- The tools to do good work (equipment, training, support)
- Honest feedback (positive and corrective)
- A leader who's not on the edge of burnout

You owe yourself:
- A business that supports your life — not the other way around
- Time and energy for family, health, and the next decade of you
- Money that flows because the company is built well, not because
  you ground out one more hour

The owner operating system is how you keep that promise.

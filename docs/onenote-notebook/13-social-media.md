# Social Media

Brand voice, posting cadence, content calendar, and templates per
platform. A consistent social presence is the second-biggest local-SEO
lever after Google Business Profile.

---

## Brand voice

PVS sounds like: **a Valley neighbour who happens to run the best
property care crew in town.**

- Friendly, not stiff
- Plain English, no jargon
- Local references (Petawawa, Pembroke, the Valley, named towns)
- Crew + mascot personality showing through (Almighty Sam earns a
  cameo regularly)
- Helpful first, salesy second
- Photos > words
- Never disparage a competitor
- Never make a promise we can't deliver

What we never do:
- Generic stock photo posts
- Hashtag spam (5 max per post, all local + service-specific)
- AI-generated cringe (the mascot is the only cartoon character;
  everything else is real)
- "Limited time only" panic posts
- Politics / unrelated commentary

---

## Platforms — priority order

1. **Google Business Profile** — highest local-SEO ROI; treat as a
   social channel with weekly posts
2. **Facebook** — Ottawa Valley audience uses Facebook heavily;
   community groups especially powerful
3. **Instagram** — visual showcase + younger audience
4. **TikTok** — high-impact for "before/after" reels; experiment if
   time allows
5. **LinkedIn** — only if pursuing commercial accounts (low priority
   for residential focus)
6. **YouTube** — long-form how-to videos can rank for SEO; aspirational

Don't try to be everywhere. Pick 2–3 and do them well.

## Posting cadence

| Platform | Cadence | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Google Business Profile | 1 post / week | Monday morning |
| Facebook | 3 posts / week | Mon, Wed, Fri |
| Instagram | 3 posts + 2 stories / week | Mon, Wed, Fri + daily stories |
| TikTok / Reels | 1–2 reels / week | Tue, Thu |
| LinkedIn | 1 post / month | First Wed of month |

Use a scheduling tool (Buffer, Later, Meta Business Suite) — never
post live. Batch a week or month of content at once.

up 3 weeks in a row.

| Slot | Content type |
|---|---|
| Monday | Before/after job photo + 1-line caption |

## Photo standards

Every job photo posted must:
- Be in focus
- Be properly lit (no over-shadowed faces, no blown-out windows)
- Show recognisable PVS branding when possible (uniform, truck,
  yard sign)
- Have customer permission if their property is identifiable (always
  ask at the handoff)
- Be cropped 1:1 (Instagram), 4:5 (Instagram portrait), 16:9
  (Facebook landscape), 9:16 (Reels / Stories)

Bad photos hurt the brand. If you wouldn't print it, don't post it.

## Engagement rules

- Respond to every comment within 24 hours
- Like every customer photo we're tagged in
- Share / repost any positive customer mention
- Never delete a negative comment — respond professionally per the
  customer-script section
- DM responses within 4 hours during business hours

## Monthly content batch (do once per month)

3-hour block, first Monday:

1. Review the prior month: top 3 posts by engagement, what worked
2. Pick a theme for the upcoming month (e.g. "Spring cleanup",
   "Snow contract season", "Window care 101")
3. Pull 20–30 candidate photos from the gallery folder
4. Draft the next month of posts in the scheduler
5. Approve + schedule

## KPIs to track monthly

- Followers per platform (vanity, but trend matters)
- Engagement rate (likes + comments / followers)
- Profile visits → website clicks (Google + Meta both expose this)
- Inbound DMs / quote requests attributed to social
- Top-performing post format (drives next month's plan)

Report to Owner first Monday of every month. Adjust strategy quarterly.

---

## Don'ts

- Don't post a competitor's logo, even to mock
- Don't post photos of customers' kids or pets without explicit
  written consent
- Don't post a customer's full address — town only
- Don't post about an in-progress dispute or complaint
- Don't post political opinions, even seemingly innocuous ones
- Don't recycle the exact same caption across multiple platforms in
  one day — tailor the language to each channel
